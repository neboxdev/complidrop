# Texas regulatory obligations — SECURITY SERVICE (contract security / guard companies working events)

Entity type: `security-service`. Scope: contract security / guard companies that
supply watchmen, guards, or event security on a contractual basis to another
person (e.g. an event venue). Jurisdiction level: **state-tx**. United States only.

Governing law: **Texas Occupations Code Chapter 1702 (Private Security)**, the
"Private Security Act," administered by the **Texas Department of Public Safety
(DPS) Private Security Program** (Regulatory Services Division). The former Texas
Private Security Board was abolished by SB 616 (86th Leg., 2019) and its functions
transferred into DPS proper; the statute now refers to "the department" (DPS) and
"the commission" (the Public Safety Commission). Administrative rules are in
**37 Texas Administrative Code, Part 1, Chapter 35**.

### OBL-TX-SECURITY-001: Security services contractor (guard company) license

- **Applies to:** Any person/entity that, on a contractual basis for another
  person, provides "a private watchman, guard, or street patrol service" — this
  squarely covers a contract security company supplying guards to an event venue.
  Also covers armored car, courier, alarm, and locksmith companies (not in scope
  here). The license class for a guard company is the **security services
  contractor license** with the **guard company** subcategory/endorsement.
- **Obligation:** Hold a company license (security services contractor license)
  issued by DPS before acting as, or offering to act as, a guard company.
- **Issuing/enforcing authority:** Texas Department of Public Safety (DPS),
  Private Security Program.
- **Cadence:** Initial license, then renewal on or before expiration — see
  OBL-TX-SECURITY-002 for the term (up to 2 years).
- **Penalty for lapse:** Operating without a required license is prohibited and
  is subject to criminal and administrative penalty under ch. 1702 — see
  OBL-TX-SECURITY-009.
- **Category:** license
- **Basis:** regulatory
- **Citation:** Tex. Occ. Code §§ 1702.102, 1702.108 — fetched from
  https://texas.public.law/statutes/tex._occ._code_section_1702.102 and
  https://texas.public.law/statutes/tex._occ._code_section_1702.108
  (citation of record: statutes.capitol.texas.gov Occupations Code ch. 1702)
- **Operative text:** § 1702.102 ("Security Services Contractor License Required;
  Scope of License") (a): "Unless the person holds a license as a security
  services contractor, a person may not: (1) act as an alarm systems company,
  armored car company, courier company, guard company, or locksmith company;
  (2) offer to perform the services of a company [in] Subdivision (1); or
  (3) engage in business activity for which a license is required under this
  chapter." § 1702.108 ("Guard Company"): "A person acts as a guard company for
  the purposes of this chapter if the person employs an individual described by
  Section 1702.323(d) or engages in the business of or undertakes to provide a
  private watchman, guard, or street patrol service on a contractual basis for
  another person to: (1) prevent entry, larceny, vandalism, abuse, fire, or
  trespass on private property; (2) prevent, observe, or detect unauthorized
  activity on private property; (3) control, regulate, or direct the movement of
  the public, whether by vehicle or otherwise, only to the extent and for the time
  directly and specifically required to ensure the protection of property;
  (4) protect an individual from bodily harm including through the use of a
  personal protection officer; or (5) perform a function similar to a function
  listed in this section."
- **Effective date of cited text:** § 1702.102 and § 1702.108 as currently
  displayed; § 1702.108 amended by Acts 2001, 77th Leg., ch. 1420, § 14.588,
  eff. Sept. 1, 2001.
- **Verified:** 2026-07-07 | **Confidence:** verified
- **Notes:** The statutory license is on the **company**, not the venue. A venue
  hiring a licensed contractor does not itself need this license. "Guard company"
  is one of several security-services-contractor subcategories; a company may hold
  multiple. Verify the exact DPS application/endorsement labels ("Class B" /
  "guard company" on the DPS application) against the live DPS site when reachable.

### OBL-TX-SECURITY-002: License / commission term and renewal (up to 2 years)

- **Applies to:** Every company license, individual license, and security officer
  commission issued under ch. 1702 (i.e. the guard-company license and every
  per-worker credential below).
- **Obligation:** Renew the license/commission on or before its expiration; DPS
  sets staggered expiration dates, capped at the second anniversary of issuance.
- **Issuing/enforcing authority:** DPS (expiration dates set by the Public Safety
  Commission under Gov't Code § 411.511, staggered renewal).
- **Cadence:** Expires on a date set by the commission **but not later than the
  second anniversary of issuance** — i.e. a term of up to two years, then renew.
- **Penalty for lapse:** Operating on an expired license/commission = unlicensed
  activity (OBL-TX-SECURITY-009); late renewal typically incurs DPS late fees
  (agency-practice, not confirmed live — see Notes).
- **Category:** license
- **Basis:** regulatory
- **Citation:** Tex. Occ. Code § 1702.301 (Expiration); cross-ref Gov't Code
  § 411.511 — fetched from
  https://texas.public.law/statutes/tex._occ._code_section_1702.301
- **Operative text:** § 1702.301(b): "A company license, individual license, and
  security officer commission expire on the dates determined by the commission
  under Section 411.511, Government Code, but not later than the second
  anniversary of the date the license or commission is issued." (c): "A personal
  protection officer license expires on the date determined by the commission
  under Section 411.511, Government Code, but not later than the expiration date
  of the security officer commission under which the license is issued." (h):
  "A license issued under this chapter, other than one specified in this section,
  expires on the date determined by the commission ... but not later than the
  second anniversary of the date the license is issued."
- **Effective date of cited text:** As currently displayed.
- **Verified:** 2026-07-07 | **Confidence:** verified
- **Notes:** Statute caps the term at two years; the exact expiration date is set
  by DPS's staggered-renewal schedule, so an individual license may run less than
  two full years. A PPO license is pinned to the underlying commission's
  expiration. Precise DPS renewal windows / late-fee amounts are agency practice
  and could not be confirmed against the down DPS site (see review queue).

### OBL-TX-SECURITY-003: Commercial general-liability insurance on file with DPS (LAW-MANDATED)

- **Applies to:** Every applicant for a **company license** under ch. 1702 —
  including a guard company. This is a statutory precondition of licensure, not a
  contract term.
- **Obligation:** Provide, as part of the license application, a certificate of
  insurance / documentary evidence of a **general liability insurance policy**
  (or surplus-lines equivalent) meeting statutory minimum limits, conditioned to
  pay damages the license holder becomes legally obligated to pay for bodily
  injury, property damage, or personal injury caused in the conduct of licensed
  activity.
- **Issuing/enforcing authority:** DPS (certificate filed with the application);
  insurer/agent must be Texas-licensed (or surplus-lines under Ins. Code ch. 981).
- **Cadence:** Required at application and must be maintained for the life of the
  license (continuous coverage; certificate on file with DPS).
- **Penalty for lapse:** Ineligibility for / loss of the company license;
  operating without the required license is prohibited (OBL-TX-SECURITY-009).
- **Category:** insurance
- **Basis:** regulatory
- **Citation:** Tex. Occ. Code § 1702.124 (Insurance) — fetched from
  https://codes.findlaw.com/tx/occupations-code/occ-sect-1702-124/ and
  https://texas.public.law/statutes/tex._occ._code_section_1702.124
- **Operative text:** § 1702.124(a): "An applicant is not eligible for a company
  license unless the applicant provides as part of the application: (1) a
  certificate of insurance or other documentary evidence of a general liability
  insurance policy countersigned by an insurance agent licensed in this state; or
  (2) a certificate of insurance for surplus lines coverage obtained under Chapter
  981, Insurance Code, through a licensed Texas surplus lines agent resident in
  this state." (b): "The general liability insurance policy must be conditioned to
  pay on behalf of the company license holder damages that the company license
  holder becomes legally obligated to pay because of bodily injury, property
  damage, or personal injury, caused by an event involving the principal, or an
  officer, agent, or employee of the principal, in the conduct of any activity or
  service for which the company license holder is licensed under this chapter."
  (c): "The insurance policy must contain minimum limits of: (1) $100,000 for each
  occurrence for bodily injury and property damage; (2) $50,000 for each
  occurrence for personal injury; and (3) a total aggregate amount of $200,000 for
  all occurrences."
- **Effective date of cited text:** As currently displayed.
- **Verified:** 2026-07-07 | **Confidence:** verified
- **Notes:** THIS is the law-mandated insurance rule (distinct from any COI a
  venue contractually requires, which is typically far higher). Statutory floor:
  **$100k/occurrence BI+PD, $50k/occurrence personal injury, $200k aggregate.**
  A venue's own contractual COI requirement (often $1M/$2M) is separate and
  `contractual-noted`, not encoded here. Subsections (d)-(f) add provisions for
  fire-alarm installers / certificate mechanics not relevant to a guard company.

### OBL-TX-SECURITY-004: Individual license — noncommissioned (unarmed) security officer

- **Applies to:** Each individual employed by the guard company to work as an
  **unarmed** security officer/guard (the typical event-security worker who does
  not carry a firearm). The worker must be employed by a company license holder.
- **Obligation:** Hold an individual license (noncommissioned security officer)
  issued by DPS and be employed by a licensed company.
- **Issuing/enforcing authority:** DPS, Private Security Program.
- **Cadence:** Term up to 2 years, then renew (OBL-TX-SECURITY-002). Requires the
  Level II training / exam before or shortly after commencing work
  (OBL-TX-SECURITY-007).
- **Penalty for lapse:** Working unlicensed, or a company using unlicensed
  personnel, is prohibited (OBL-TX-SECURITY-009).
- **Category:** worker-certification
- **Basis:** regulatory
- **Citation:** Tex. Occ. Code § 1702.221 (Individual License Required) — fetched
  from https://texas.public.law/statutes/tex._occ._code_section_1702.221
- **Operative text:** § 1702.221: an individual must "(1) obtain the proper
  individual license under Subsection (b); and (2) be employed by a company
  license holder." Subsection (b) enumerates the individual-license categories,
  which include **"noncommissioned security officers,"** "classroom or firearm
  instructors (level 3)," "personal protection instructors (level 4)," locksmiths,
  private investigators, alarm installers/monitors, and an owner/officer holding
  51% or more of a license holder; and "the commission by rule may require a
  person to hold an individual license for performing any other activity expressly
  regulated by this chapter."
- **Effective date of cited text:** As currently displayed (reflects SB 616,
  86th Leg. (2019), which converted former "registrations" to "individual
  licenses" and moved the program into DPS).
- **Verified:** 2026-07-07 | **Confidence:** verified
- **Notes:** Full verbatim enumeration of subsection (b) categories should be
  re-pulled from the live statute when reachable to confirm the exact list wording
  (the fetched reproduction summarized the list). The credential-holder is the
  individual worker; the employing company must also be licensed (OBL-...-001).

### OBL-TX-SECURITY-005: Security officer commission — armed (commissioned) security officer

- **Applies to:** Each individual employed as a security officer who will **carry
  a firearm** in the course and scope of duties (armed event security).
- **Obligation:** Hold a **security officer commission** issued by DPS (in
  addition to being employed by a licensed company); a company may not employ an
  armed security officer who lacks a commission.
- **Issuing/enforcing authority:** DPS, Private Security Program.
- **Cadence:** Term up to 2 years, then renew (OBL-TX-SECURITY-002). Requires
  Level III (commissioned/handgun) training (OBL-TX-SECURITY-008).
- **Penalty for lapse:** Carrying a firearm on duty without a commission, or a
  company employing such a person, is prohibited (OBL-TX-SECURITY-009).
- **Category:** worker-certification
- **Basis:** regulatory
- **Citation:** Tex. Occ. Code § 1702.161 (Security Officer Commission Required)
  — fetched from https://texas.public.law/statutes/tex._occ._code_section_1702.161
- **Operative text:** § 1702.161(a): "An individual may not accept employment as a
  security officer to carry a firearm in the course and scope of the individual's
  duties unless the individual holds a security officer commission." (b): "An
  individual employed as a security officer may not knowingly carry a firearm
  during the course of performing duties as a security officer unless the
  department has issued a security officer commission to the individual." (c): "A
  person may not hire or employ an individual as a security officer to carry a
  firearm in the course and scope of the individual's duties unless the individual
  holds a security officer commission."
- **Effective date of cited text:** As currently displayed.
- **Verified:** 2026-07-07 | **Confidence:** verified
- **Notes:** The commission is the "armed" credential; an unarmed guard needs only
  the OBL-...-004 individual license. Commissioned officers must complete Level III
  handgun training and qualify (OBL-...-008).

### OBL-TX-SECURITY-006: Personal protection officer (PPO) authorization

- **Applies to:** An individual who, while carrying a firearm, provides personal
  protection from bodily harm to another individual (armed bodyguard / close
  protection — a guard-company service under § 1702.108(4)).
- **Obligation:** Hold a personal protection officer license/authorization, which
  is layered on top of a security officer commission.
- **Issuing/enforcing authority:** DPS, Private Security Program.
- **Cadence:** PPO license expires no later than the expiration of the underlying
  security officer commission (§ 1702.301(c)); requires Level IV (PPO) training.
- **Penalty for lapse:** Acting as a PPO without authorization is prohibited
  (OBL-TX-SECURITY-009).
- **Category:** worker-certification
- **Basis:** regulatory
- **Citation:** Tex. Occ. Code §§ 1702.201, 1702.202; term/prereq § 1702.301(c) —
  fetched from https://texas.public.law/statutes/tex._occ._code_section_1702.201 ,
  https://texas.public.law/statutes/tex._occ._code_section_1702.202 and
  https://texas.public.law/statutes/tex._occ._code_section_1702.301
- **Operative text:** § 1702.201 ("Personal Protection Officer License Required"):
  "An individual may not act as a personal protection officer unless the
  individual holds a personal protection officer license." § 1702.202 ("Personal
  Protection Officer"): "An individual acts as a personal protection officer if
  the individual, while carrying a firearm, provides to another individual
  personal protection from bodily harm." § 1702.301(c): "A personal protection
  officer license expires on the date determined by the commission ... but not
  later than the expiration date of the security officer commission under which
  the license is issued."
- **Effective date of cited text:** As currently displayed; § 1702.202 amended by
  Acts 2009, 81st Leg., R.S., ch. 1146 (H.B. 2730), § 4B.12, eff. Sept. 1, 2009;
  § 1702.201 amended by Acts 2019, 86th Leg., R.S., ch. 595 (S.B. 616).
- **Verified:** 2026-07-07 | **Confidence:** verified
- **Notes:** A PPO is an ARMED credential issued *under* a security officer
  commission — § 1702.301(c) pins the PPO license's expiration to "the security
  officer commission under which the license is issued," so a PPO must also hold a
  commission (OBL-TX-SECURITY-005) and complete Level IV training
  (OBL-TX-SECURITY-008). Most event-security work does not involve PPOs; included
  for completeness. The exact commission-first prerequisite wording (§ 1702.163 /
  § 1702.203) was not pulled verbatim, but the license-required rule and the
  issued-under-commission linkage are both verified above.

### OBL-TX-SECURITY-007: Level II training — noncommissioned security officers (and commission applicants)

- **Applies to:** Every applicant for a noncommissioned (unarmed) security officer
  individual license, and every applicant for a security officer commission. This
  is the baseline training the typical unarmed event-security guard must complete.
- **Obligation:** Complete the DPS Level II training course (and its test) before
  the individual license/commission issues (or within the window DPS allows).
- **Issuing/enforcing authority:** DPS, Private Security Program (course delivered
  by a licensed company designee or a DPS-approved school).
- **Cadence:** One-time pre-credential requirement (tied to the individual license
  / commission in OBL-TX-SECURITY-004 / -005).
- **Penalty for lapse:** An untrained/uncredentialed guard working = unlicensed
  activity (OBL-TX-SECURITY-009).
- **Category:** worker-certification
- **Basis:** regulatory
- **Citation:** 37 Tex. Admin. Code § 35.141 (Training Requirements), Subchapter L
  — fetched from http://txrules.elaws.us/rule/title37_chapter35_sec.35.141
  (citation of record: SOS Texas Administrative Code, texreg.sos.state.tx.us,
  Title 37 Part 1 ch. 35)
- **Operative text:** § 35.141: "The Level II training course shall be completed
  by all applicants for a security officer commission or for a license as a
  noncommissioned security officer."
- **Effective date of cited text:** Current as displayed on the fetched TAC
  reproduction (see Notes re: hour count).
- **Verified:** 2026-07-07 | **Confidence:** verified
- **Notes:** The APPLICABILITY (who must take Level II) is quoted verbatim and

### OBL-TX-SECURITY-008: Level III training (commissioned/armed officers) and Level IV training (PPOs)

- **Applies to:** Level III — every applicant for a security officer commission
  (armed guard) and every PPO applicant. Level IV — every applicant for a personal
  protection officer license.
- **Obligation:** Complete the DPS Level III handgun/commissioned-officer training
  course and test (including course of fire) for a commission; additionally
  complete the Level IV course for a PPO license.
- **Issuing/enforcing authority:** DPS (Level III/IV delivered by DPS-approved
  schools and instructors).
- **Cadence:** Pre-credential; commissioned officers and PPOs must also complete
  periodic continuing education (see Notes — count not verified live).
- **Penalty for lapse:** Carrying a firearm on duty without a valid commission (or
  acting as a PPO without a license) = prohibited (OBL-TX-SECURITY-009).
- **Category:** worker-certification
- **Basis:** regulatory
- **Citation:** 37 Tex. Admin. Code § 35.141 (Training Requirements); handgun
  course § 35.145 — fetched from
  http://txrules.elaws.us/rule/title37_chapter35_sec.35.141
- **Operative text:** § 35.141: "The Level III training course shall be completed
  by all applicants for a security officer commission and a personal protection
  officer license." "The Level IV training course shall be completed by all
  applicants for a personal protection officer license." (Level IV) "a minimum of
  fifteen (15) classroom hours."
- **Effective date of cited text:** Current as displayed on the fetched TAC
  reproduction.
- **Verified:** 2026-07-07 | **Confidence:** verified
- **Notes:** Applicability of Level III and Level IV, and the **Level IV 15-hour**
  minimum, are quoted verbatim and verified. The **Level III minimum hour count
  (a DPS training page reports ~45 hours) and the ~6-hour continuing-education

### OBL-TX-SECURITY-009: Penalty — unlicensed / uncommissioned activity is a crime

- **Applies to:** Any person acting as, or employing someone to act as, a security
  services contractor / guard company, security officer, commissioned officer, or
  PPO without the required license, commission, or certificate of insurance.
- **Obligation:** (Enforcement provision, not a filing.) Establishes the criminal
  penalty backing every ch. 1702 credential and the § 1702.124 insurance mandate.
- **Issuing/enforcing authority:** DPS + county/district prosecutors; DPS also has
  administrative sanctions (denial/suspension/revocation, administrative penalty).
- **Cadence:** N/A (penalty).
- **Penalty for lapse:** **Class A misdemeanor**, elevated to a **third-degree
  felony** on a prior conviction under the chapter for failing to hold a required
  license, certificate of insurance, or commission.
- **Category:** filing (enforcement/penalty reference)
- **Basis:** regulatory
- **Citation:** Tex. Occ. Code § 1702.388 (Violation of Chapter; Offense) —
  fetched from https://codes.findlaw.com/tx/occupations-code/occ-sect-1702-388/
  (citation of record: statutes.capitol.texas.gov Occupations Code ch. 1702)
- **Operative text:** § 1702.388: "(a) A person commits an offense if the person
  violates a provision of this chapter for which a specific criminal penalty is
  not prescribed. (b) An offense under this section is a Class A misdemeanor,
  except that the offense is a felony of the third degree if the person has
  previously been convicted under this chapter of failing to hold a license,
  certificate of insurance, or commission that the person is required to hold under
  this chapter."
- **Effective date of cited text:** As currently displayed.
- **Verified:** 2026-07-07 | **Confidence:** verified
- **Notes:** This is the enforcement teeth behind OBL-TX-SECURITY-001 through -008
  and the § 1702.124 insurance requirement. Chapter 1702 also carries a separate
  civil penalty and injunctive relief (e.g. §§ 1702.381–1702.386) not pulled
  verbatim this session; the criminal offense above is the primary lapse penalty.

## Local-level obligations (noted, not encoded)

- **Municipal peace-officer / off-duty officer rules.** Many event venues use
  off-duty police officers rather than (or alongside) licensed private guards.
  Off-duty peace officers acting under a city's extra-employment policy operate
  under municipal/agency rules, not ch. 1702, and are a separate regime — note but
  do not encode.
- **City alarm-permit / special-event security ordinances.** Some Texas cities
  require special-event permits that specify minimum security staffing (e.g. for
  large assemblies or alcohol events); these are city ordinances, not ch. 1702.
  "Check your city/county."
- **County/city occupancy & fire-marshal crowd-management rules** may dictate the
  NUMBER of guards for an event but do not change the state licensing of the guard
  company. Out of encoding scope.
