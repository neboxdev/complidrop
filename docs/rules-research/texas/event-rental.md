# Texas regulatory obligations — Event Rental Company

Entity: **event-rental** (tables, tents, chairs, staging, equipment, and
inflatables / bounce houses rented to event venues). Jurisdiction level:
**state-tx**. Market: United States only.

> **Headline finding (corrects a research lead):** Texas amusement-ride
> regulation (Occupations Code ch. 2151) is administered by the **Texas
> Department of Insurance (TDI) / Commissioner of Insurance — NOT TDLR.** Every
> current TDI page and the statute itself route filings to "the commissioner"
> and "the department" (= TDI). The lead's "administered by TDLR" hypothesis is
> **refuted**. TDLR has no role in amusement-ride insurance/inspection.
>
> **Highest-value obligation:** an event-rental company that rents
> **continuous air-flow inflatables (bounce houses, inflatable slides)** is
> operating **amusement rides** and owes the full ch. 2151 / 28 TAC ch. 5
> subch. J regime: mandatory liability insurance, annual inspection, TDI
> compliance sticker, quarterly injury reports. Non-compliance is a **Class B
> misdemeanor**.

### OBL-TX-EVENT-001: Mandatory amusement-ride liability insurance (inflatables / bounce houses)

- **Applies to:** Any person who owns, controls, manages, or leases an
  **amusement ride** for use in Texas. Includes **continuous air-flow
  inflatable rides/devices (bounce houses, inflatable slides)** — TDI expressly
  lists these as amusement rides. A rented event inflatable is a **Class B**
  ride (Class A is a *fixed-location* ride designed primarily for children ≤12;
  a mobile rental inflatable is not fixed-location, so it is Class B). **Not**
  triggered for non-mechanical inventory (tables, chairs, staging, linens) or
  for a vinyl waterslide under 200 ft that has **no** continuous mechanical
  airflow (statutorily excluded — see Notes).
- **Obligation:** Maintain a **combined single limit or split limit liability
  insurance policy currently in effect**, written by an insurer authorized in
  Texas. For a **continuous-airflow inflatable (bounce house / inflatable slide)**
  the **controlling, more-specific** minimum is set by **Tex. Occ. Code
  §2151.1012**: a **combined single limit policy of not less than $1,000,000 per
  occurrence** (specific-controls-general — distinct from, and lower than, the
  general Class B $1.5M CSL alternative). The general Class B floor of
  §2151.101(a)(3)(B) — **$1,000,000 bodily injury and $500,000 property damage per
  occurrence, or $1,500,000 per occurrence combined single limit** — applies to
  **non-inflatable** Class B rides. A certified copy of the policy is filed with
  TDI (see OBL-TX-EVENT-002).
- **Issuing/enforcing authority:** Texas Department of Insurance (TDI) /
  Commissioner of Insurance (Amusement Ride Program).
- **Cadence:** Policy must be *currently in effect* at all times the ride
  operates. Insurance policy + inspection certificate filed with TDI **before
  July 1 of each year** (statute); compliance sticker is valid one year from the
  inspection date (TDI practice). Operator must give TDI ≥10 working days'
  notice before either document expires (28 TAC §5.9004).
- **Penalty for lapse:** Operating without the required insurance/inspection is
  a **Class B misdemeanor** (Occ. Code §2151.153); law enforcement / TDI may
  immediately prohibit operation of a non-compliant ride.
- **Category:** insurance
- **Basis:** regulatory
- **Citation:** Tex. Occ. Code **§2151.1012** (inflatable-specific — continuous-
  airflow rides, $1M per-occurrence CSL) and **§2151.101(a)(3)** (general Class A/B
  minimums), §2151.002 (definition); 28 TAC §5.9004(b) mirrors the statutory dollar
  minimums.
  - Statute read at: https://statutes.capitol.texas.gov/Docs/OC/htm/OC.2151.htm
    (OFFICIAL host statutes.capitol.texas.gov, rendered via Playwright this session)
  - Rule corroboration: https://www.law.cornell.edu/regulations/texas/28-Tex-Admin-Code-SS-5-9004
    and http://txrules.elaws.us/rule/title28_chapter5_sec.5.9004 (both current;
    txrules shows last amended **Nov 3, 2024, 49 TexReg 8587**)
  - Agency corroboration: https://www.tdi.texas.gov/commercial/lcamuseinfo.html
    (TDI Amusement Ride FAQ)
- **Operative text:**
  - Occ. Code **§2151.1012** (inflatable-specific, CONTROLLING for bounce houses):
    "**This section applies only to a Class B amusement ride that: (1) is
    mechanically inflated using a continuous airflow device; and (2) provides a
    surface for bouncing and jumping or creates an enclosed space for the purpose of
    amusement. (b) A person may not operate an amusement ride described by Subsection
    (a) unless the person has a combined single limit insurance policy ... in an
    amount of not less than $1 million per occurrence.**"
  - Occ. Code **§2151.101(a)(3)** (general Class A/B minimums, CONTEXT for
    non-inflatable rides): "**(A) for Class A amusement rides: (i) $100,000 bodily
    injury and $50,000 property damage per occurrence with a $300,000 annual
    aggregate; or (ii) a $150,000 per occurrence combined single limit with a
    $300,000 annual aggregate; and (B) for Class B amusement rides: (i) $1,000,000
    bodily injury and $500,000 property damage per occurrence; or (ii) $1,500,000 per
    occurrence combined single limit;**"
  - Occ. Code §2151.101(a): an operator must have "**a combined single limit or
    split limit insurance policy currently in effect written by an insurance
    company authorized to do business in this state**."
  - TDI FAQ (device coverage): amusement rides include "**continuous air-flow
    inflatable rides or devices (bounce houses, inflatable slides)**."
- **Effective date of cited text:** Occ. Code §2151.101 — current as displayed
  (act eff. 1999, amended through 2011). 28 TAC §5.9004 — last amended Nov 3,
  2024 (49 TexReg 8587), current as displayed.

### OBL-TX-EVENT-002: Annual amusement-ride inspection certificate + TDI compliance sticker (Form AR-101)

- **Applies to:** Same population as OBL-TX-EVENT-001 (operators of amusement
  rides, incl. rented inflatables/bounce houses).
- **Obligation:** (1) Have each ride **inspected at least once a year** by the
  insurer or a person the insurer contracts with; (2) **file the inspection
  certificate and the insurance policy** (or photocopy) **with TDI**; (3) affix
  the **TDI Form AR-101 Amusement Ride Compliance Sticker** TDI issues on
  approval to a major, rider-visible component of each ride. Operator must also
  furnish the certificate/policy copy to the sponsor, lessor, or landowner where
  the ride is set up.
- **Issuing/enforcing authority:** Texas Department of Insurance (TDI),
  Amusement Ride Program.
- **Cadence:** Inspection **at least once a year**; certificate + policy filed
  with TDI **before July 1 of each year** (statute); sticker valid **one year
  from the inspection date** (TDI practice); ≥10 working days' notice to TDI
  before expiry. TDI charges a **$40 filing fee per ride**.
- **Penalty for lapse:** Class B misdemeanor (Occ. Code §2151.153); operation
  of an uninspected/unstickered ride may be immediately prohibited.
- **Category:** permit (compliance credential / inspection certificate)
- **Basis:** regulatory
- **Citation:** Tex. Occ. Code §2151.101(a)(1)–(4) & (deadline clause); 28 TAC
  §5.9004 (sticker).
  - Statute: https://texas.public.law/statutes/tex._occ._code_section_2151.101
  - TDI adopted rule (sticker text): https://www.tdi.texas.gov/rules/2007/0215-059.html
  - TDI program overview / $40 fee: https://www.tdi.texas.gov/commercial/indexamusement.html
- **Operative text:**
  - §2151.101(a): operator must have "**had the amusement ride inspected at
    least once a year by an insurer or a person with whom the insurer has
    contracted**," and "**files with the commissioner, as required by this
    chapter, the inspection certificate and the insurance policy or a
    photocopy**."
  - §2151.101: "**The inspection certificate and the insurance policy must be
    filed with the department before July 1 of each year.**"
  - 28 TAC §5.9004 (sticker): "**TDI Form AR-101 (Texas Amusement Ride
    Compliance Sticker) … will indicate the expiration date of the inspection
    certificate and shall be affixed to a major component of the amusement ride
    in a location visible to the ride participants.**"
- **Effective date of cited text:** Statute current as displayed; sticker rule
  (AR-101 form) effective May 2000, adopted by reference, current as displayed.
- **Verified:** 2026-07-07 | **Confidence:** verified
- **Notes:** Tension to reconcile — the statute states a **before-July-1 annual**
  filing, while TDI operational materials describe a **rolling one-year-from-
  inspection** sticker validity. Both are recorded; a founder should confirm the
  current annual-cycle mechanics with TDI (review queue). The $40/ride fee is
  from the TDI program page (agency), not the statute.

### OBL-TX-EVENT-003: Amusement-ride quarterly injury report (Form AR-800)

- **Applies to:** Amusement-ride owners/operators (incl. inflatable rentals)
  when a ride causes a reportable injury.
- **Obligation:** Submit a **Quarterly Injury Report** to the Commissioner for
  any injury caused by an amusement ride that **results in death or requires
  medical treatment** (first aid / minor injury excluded). No reportable
  incidents in a quarter ⇒ no report due.
- **Issuing/enforcing authority:** Texas Department of Insurance (Commissioner).
- **Cadence:** **Quarterly.**
- **Penalty for lapse:** Failure to report is enumerated among the §2151.153
  offenses — **Class B misdemeanor.**
- **Category:** filing
- **Basis:** regulatory
- **Citation:** Tex. Occ. Code §2151.103 (Injury Reports); TDI Form AR-800.
  - Statute: https://texas.public.law/statutes/tex._occ._code_section_2151.103
  - Form/practice: https://www.tdi.texas.gov/commercial/lcamuseinfo.html
- **Operative text:** §2151.103 requires operators to report injuries that
  "**result in death or require medical treatment**," filed "**on a quarterly
  basis**" to "**the commissioner**." TDI: "**Amusement ride owners and
  operators must submit a Quarterly Injury Report (Form AR-800) for any injuries
  caused by an amusement ride.**"
- **Effective date of cited text:** §2151.103 current as displayed (eff. 1999,
  amended 2001).
- **Verified:** 2026-07-07 | **Confidence:** verified
- **Notes:** This is a conditional/event-driven filing, not a periodic license.
  Relevant to CompliDrop only as a recurring obligation the vendor must be able
  to demonstrate; the artifact is a filed report, not a credential.

### OBL-TX-EVENT-004: (Absence) No Texas general state business license

- **Applies to:** All event-rental companies operating in Texas.
- **Obligation:** **None at the state level** — Texas does not issue a general
  business license. A company still registers its entity (SOS) or assumed name
  (county), obtains a sales/use tax permit if it rents taxable tangible property
  (see cross-refs), and meets any *local* license requirements.
- **Issuing/enforcing authority:** N/A (absence). Governor's Office Business
  Permit Office publishes the state permit guide.
- **Cadence:** N/A.
- **Penalty for lapse:** N/A.
- **Category:** license (absence finding)
- **Basis:** regulatory
- **Citation:** Texas Governor's Office — Business Permit Office.
  - https://gov.texas.gov/business/page/business-permits-office
- **Operative text:** "**The State of Texas does not require a general
  license.**" The office adds that "**all permitting requirements are also met
  at the local level**" and directs businesses to "**contact your local county
  and city government**."
- **Effective date of cited text:** Current as displayed (references the
  2026–2027 Texas Business Licenses & Permits Guide).
- **Verified:** 2026-07-07 | **Confidence:** verified
- **Notes:** Absence-is-a-finding entry so the engine distinguishes "no state
  license exists" from "not researched." Activity-specific state credentials
  (e.g., the amusement-ride regime above) still apply.

### OBL-TX-EVENT-005: (Absence at state level) No Texas state tent / temporary-structure permit — local fire code only

- **Applies to:** Event-rental companies that supply/erect **tents, canopies,
  and membrane structures**.
- **Obligation:** **No state-level tent permit exists.** Tents and membrane
  structures are regulated **locally** under each city/county's adopted &
  amended **International Fire Code (IFC) Chapter 31**. A permit is typically
  required for a tent/membrane structure **over 400 sq ft** (or any size cooking
  tent), with flame-resistance (NFPA 701) certification, egress, extinguisher,
  and fire-break requirements. Thresholds/exemptions vary by jurisdiction.
- **Issuing/enforcing authority:** **Local** city/county fire marshal (not a
  state agency). Texas has no statewide tent-permit program.
- **Cadence:** Per-event / per-installation, set locally.
- **Penalty for lapse:** Local (stop-work / fine), set by the adopting
  ordinance.
- **Category:** permit (state-level absence; local obligation noted)
- **Basis:** regulatory
- **Citation:** No Texas statute/agency issues a state tent permit (see
  OBL-TX-EVENT-004 local-referral quote). Local rule of record is the model IFC
  Ch. 31 as locally adopted.
  - State-absence anchor (fetched, approved primary): https://gov.texas.gov/business/page/business-permits-office
  - Illustrative local rule (NOT fetched as primary this session; from search
    summary): model IFC 2021 Ch. 31 —
    https://codes.iccsafe.org/content/IFC2021P1/chapter-31-tents-temporary-special-event-structures-and-other-membrane-structures ;
    example local implementation (Fort Worth FMO tent permit):
    https://www.fortworthtexas.gov/files/assets/public/v/1/development-services/documents/resources-applications-forms-videos/p/tent-permit-2023.pdf
- **Operative text (state-level absence):** Texas Governor's Business Permit
  Office: "**The State of Texas does not require a general license,**" and
  "**all permitting requirements are also met at the local level**" — directing
  businesses to "**contact your local county and city government**." No Texas
  statute or state agency issues a tent / temporary-structure permit; the
  authority is local. *(The commonly cited "over 400 sq ft ⇒ local fire permit"
  IFC Ch. 31 threshold is illustrative and was read from a search summary, not
  fetched verbatim from a primary source this session — treat as local color,
  not an encoded rule.)*
- **Effective date of cited text:** gov.texas.gov current as displayed; IFC 2021
  edition; local adoption dates vary.
- **Verified:** 2026-07-07 | **Confidence:** verified (state-level absence is
  anchored to the fetched gov.texas.gov statement + the structural fact that no
  state agency issues tent permits); local thresholds vary by jurisdiction and
  are noted, not encoded.
- **Notes:** The 400 sq ft threshold and the "open on all sides ≤700 sq ft"
  exemption are jurisdiction-specific examples, not a uniform statewide rule, and
  were not fetched as primary text. Encode only the state-level absence; surface
  a "check your city/county fire marshal" prompt for tents.

## Cross-references (owned by other entity dossiers — noted, not encoded here)

- **Texas sales & use tax permit (Comptroller).** Renting/leasing tangible
  personal property (tables, chairs, tents, staging, inflatables) is a taxable
  activity, so an event-rental company **does** need a sales-and-use-tax permit
  from the Texas Comptroller. Per research scope this obligation's **canonical
  entry is owned by `venue-org`**; cross-referenced here, not duplicated.
  Source seen: https://gov.texas.gov/business/page/business-permits-office
  ("you may need … a sales and use tax permit from the Comptroller's office").
- **Workers' compensation is OPTIONAL in Texas (Labor Code ch. 406).** Texas is
  the only state where private employers may decline workers' comp
  (non-subscriber). No coverage is legally *mandated*. Canonical entry owned by
  `venue-org`; noted here only so the engine never asserts a Texas WC mandate
  for this vendor type.
- **Commercial motor vehicle / DOT.** If the rental company operates commercial
  vehicles (box trucks etc.) to deliver inventory, TxDMV/DOT and (for
  qualifying weights/interstate) FMCSA obligations may attach. See the
  **transportation** entity research; treat as conditional, do not duplicate.

## Local-level obligations (noted, not encoded)

- **Tent / canopy / membrane-structure permits** — city/county fire marshal
  under locally adopted IFC Ch. 31 (typ. >400 sq ft; NFPA 701 flame cert,
  egress, extinguishers, fire breaks). See OBL-TX-EVENT-005.
- **Local business license / registration & certificate of occupancy** for the
  rental company's warehouse/yard (varies by city; Texas has no state analog).
- **Local fire-marshal inspection / permit for inflatables at a specific event
  venue** — some municipalities require on-site sign-off for
  bounce-house/inflatable setups at public events (in addition to the state TDI
  sticker). Check per venue city.
- **Special-event / temporary-use permits** issued by the host city for the
  event itself (usually the venue's or event organizer's burden, not the rental
  vendor's — but the vendor's tents/inflatables feed those permits).
