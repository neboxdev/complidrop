# Texas regulatory obligations — CATERER (food & beverage catering serving event venues)

Entity type: `caterer`. Jurisdiction: State of Texas (`state-tx`). United States only.
Scope per METHODOLOGY.md: business/activity licenses & permits, per-worker
certifications, insurance where mandated by law. Municipal/county obligations are
noted (not encoded) in the trailing section.

Applicability anchor: a caterer is a "food establishment" under the Texas Food
Establishment Rules. 25 TAC §228.2(14)(A)(i) defines a food establishment to
include a "catering operation if the operation provides food directly to a
consumer or to a conveyance used to transport people." A caterer serving guests
at an event venue is therefore squarely within the Texas retail-food regime.

### OBL-TX-CATERER-001: DSHS Retail Food Establishment permit (state permit where no county/health-district permit applies)

- **Applies to:** Any person operating a food service establishment (incl. a
  catering operation) located in an area where a county or public health district
  does NOT require a permit or conduct inspections. Where a county/city/public
  health district DOES permit and inspect (the common case for caterers based at
  a fixed commissary in an incorporated city or a county with a health
  department), the applicable permit is the LOCAL one — see "Local-level
  obligations" below. Statewide, a caterer that prepares/serves food needs a food
  establishment permit from SOME authority; the state (DSHS) permit is the
  residual/last-resort permit.
- **Obligation:** Hold a current DSHS retail food establishment permit (a
  Type/food-establishment permit) issued by the Texas Department of State Health
  Services for the establishment.
- **Issuing/enforcing authority:** Texas Department of State Health Services
  (DSHS), Retail Food Operations (or the county/public health district where one
  requires permits).
- **Cadence:** Biennial. §437.0055(b): the person "must apply every two years for
  the permit." Fees are charged every two years (§437.0125(b)). (A county or
  public health district that permits locally "may require the annual renewal of
  a permit" — §437.008 — so LOCAL renewal is often annual; the DSHS state permit
  is two-year.)
- **Penalty for lapse:** Operating without a required DSHS permit is a Class A
  misdemeanor; each day of violation is a separate offense (§437.0165). DSHS may
  also seek an injunction (§437.0155) and assess administrative penalties
  (§437.018).
- **Category:** permit
- **Basis:** regulatory
- **Citation:** Tex. Health & Safety Code §437.0055 (permit-required residual
  rule); §437.001(4) (definitions deferred to rule); §437.0125 (department fees,
  biennial); §437.0165 (criminal penalty); and 25 TAC §228.2(14) (TFER definition
  of "food establishment" incl. catering operation). Fetched official statute
  text at https://statutes.capitol.texas.gov/Docs/HS/htm/HS.437.htm ; TFER text at
  https://www.dshs.texas.gov/sites/default/files/foodestablishments/pdf/GuidanceDocs/TFER-2021_TAC-228_August-2021.pdf
- **Operative text:** §437.0055(a): "A person may not operate a food service
  establishment, retail food store, or temporary food service establishment
  located in an area in which a county or public health district does not require
  a permit or conduct inspections under this chapter unless the person has a
  permit issued by the department." §437.0055(b): "A person required to obtain a
  permit under Subsection (a) must apply every two years for the permit and must
  pay any fees required by the department." §437.001(4): "'Food,' 'food service
  establishment,' 'retail food store,' 'mobile food unit,' 'roadside food
  vendor,' and 'temporary food service establishment' have the meanings assigned
  to those terms by rules adopted under this chapter." 25 TAC §228.2(14)(A)(i):
  a food establishment is an operation that "stores, prepares, packages, serves,
  or vends food directly to the consumer... such as a restaurant, retail food
  store, satellite or catered feeding location, catering operation if the
  operation provides food directly to a consumer or to a conveyance used to
  transport people..."
- **Effective date of cited text:** Current as displayed (codified §437.0055 fetched
  via Playwright on statutes.capitol.texas.gov, 2026-07-07). Note: §437.0055(a) was
  amended by Acts 2025, 89th Leg., R.S., Ch. 744 (H.B. 2844), §3, eff. **July 1,
  2026**, which **STRUCK "mobile food unit"** from Subsection (a). As of 2026-07-01
  this residual §437.0055 permit therefore governs FIXED food service
  establishments, retail food stores, and temporary food service establishments
  ONLY — **mobile food units / food trucks are no longer permitted under this
  scheme**; they are now licensed statewide by DSHS under the new H&S ch. 437B (see
  OBL-TX-CATERER-007). The operative-text quote above already reflects the
  post-amendment (current in-force) wording, and the biennial-application cadence is
  unchanged.
- **Verified:** 2026-07-07 | **Confidence:** verified
- **Notes:** The statute uses "food service establishment"; the implementing rule
  (TFER, 25 TAC ch. 228) uses "food establishment" — operatively equivalent. The
  exact DSHS permit fee amount is set by executive-commissioner rule
  (§437.0125(c) references Gov't Code §12.0111) and by the DSHS fee schedule, not
  fixed in statute — fee tier not quoted here (see Open questions). For the MANY
  caterers operating in cities/counties that DO permit and inspect, the operative
  permit is the LOCAL city/county/public-health-district food permit (noted, not
  encoded). §437.006 prohibits requiring more than one permit for the same
  establishment.

### OBL-TX-CATERER-002: Certified Food Protection Manager (CFM) on staff / present

- **Applies to:** Every food establishment (incl. a catering operation) that is
  NOT within a minimal-risk exemption (prepackaged-only; no exposed
  Time/Temperature Control for Safety food; temporary food establishments). A
  caterer preparing/handling exposed TCS food must have a certified food
  protection manager.
- **Obligation:** Employ/designate at least one Certified Food Protection Manager,
  present during all hours of operation, holding a certificate from an accredited
  (state-approved-exam) food-manager certification program; the original
  certificate must be posted conspicuously to consumers.
- **Issuing/enforcing authority:** DSHS (rule + program accreditation) and the
  local regulatory authority that inspects the establishment. Certification is
  earned by passing a state-approved exam from an accredited provider (e.g.
  ANSI-CFP / ANAB-accredited programs such as ServSafe, National Registry, etc.).
- **Cadence:** Initial: pass an accredited food-manager certification exam.
  Renewal: by re-examination (Tex. H&S Code §438.103(a): a person "may receive and
  renew a food manager certificate by passing a state-approved examination"). The
  certificate's validity period is governed by the Conference for Food Protection
  (CFP) Standards §7.3 as referenced by 25 TAC §229.176 — commonly a 5-year term;
  the exact period is set by that external standard, not by statute (see Notes).
- **Penalty for lapse:** Enforced as a food establishment rule violation under
  H&S Code ch. 437 (inspection findings, administrative penalty §437.018,
  suspension/revocation §437.014). No separate CFM-specific criminal penalty
  quoted.
- **Category:** worker-certification
- **Basis:** regulatory
- **Citation:** 25 TAC §228.31(a),(b) (TFER — CFM present + certificate posted);
  Tex. Health & Safety Code §§438.101–438.103, 438.106 (Subchapter G,
  Certification of Food Managers); §437.0076 (CFM authorization). TFER text
  fetched at https://www.dshs.texas.gov/sites/default/files/foodestablishments/pdf/GuidanceDocs/TFER-2021_TAC-228_August-2021.pdf ;
  statute at https://statutes.capitol.texas.gov/Docs/HS/htm/HS.438.htm
- **Operative text:** 25 TAC §228.31(b): "Except as specified in subsection (c)
  of this section, a certified food protection manager shall be present at the
  food establishment during all hours of operation as required in Food Code,
  §2-101.11 and §2-102.12." 25 TAC §228.31(a): "The original food manager
  certificate shall be posted in the food establishment in a location that is
  conspicuous to consumers." Tex. H&S Code §438.103(a): "A person who satisfies
  the requirements of this subchapter may receive and renew a food manager
  certificate by passing a state-approved examination."
- **Effective date of cited text:** Current as displayed (TFER Aug 2021 edition;
  statute fetched 2026-07-07).
- **Verified:** 2026-07-07 | **Confidence:** verified
  (requirement + presence rule verified; the exact 5-year certificate validity is
  `probable` — set by the referenced CFP standard, not quoted here)
- **Notes:** §228.31(c) exempts minimal-risk operations (prepackaged-only; no
  exposed TCS; temporary food establishments) from the CFM-present requirement.
  The 5-year validity is a widely-cited industry figure from CFP Standard §7.3 but
  was not fetched verbatim in-session — treat the specific cadence number as
  probable pending a fetch of 25 TAC §229.176 / the CFP standard. The requirement
  to HAVE a certified manager is verified.

### OBL-TX-CATERER-003: Food Handler training/certification for food employees

- **Applies to:** All food employees of a food establishment (incl. a caterer),
  EXCEPT the certified food protection manager. Does NOT apply to temporary food
  establishments.
- **Obligation:** Each food employee must successfully complete an accredited
  food handler training course within 30 days of employment; the establishment
  must keep a certificate of completion on premises for each food employee.
- **Issuing/enforcing authority:** DSHS (accredits the food handler programs;
  25 TAC §229.178) and the local regulatory authority that inspects. Cards are
  issued by DSHS-accredited or ANSI-accredited food handler training programs.
- **Cadence:** Complete within 30 days of hire (25 TAC §228.31(d)). Certificate
  validity: two years (25 TAC §229.178(d)(1): "A food handler certificate issued
  by an accredited food handler program shall be valid for two years"). Course
  length may not exceed two hours (25 TAC §229.178(c)(2)).
- **Penalty for lapse:** Enforced as a food establishment rule violation under
  H&S Code ch. 437 (inspection findings; administrative penalty §437.018).
- **Category:** worker-certification
- **Basis:** regulatory
- **Citation:** 25 TAC §228.31(d),(e) (TFER — 30-day training + on-premises
  recordkeeping); 25 TAC §229.178(c)(2),(d)(1) (2-hour max, 2-year validity);
  Tex. Health & Safety Code §438.046 (statewide recognition of accredited food
  handler cards). TFER text fetched at
  https://www.dshs.texas.gov/sites/default/files/foodestablishments/pdf/GuidanceDocs/TFER-2021_TAC-228_August-2021.pdf ;
  25 TAC §229.178 at https://www.law.cornell.edu/regulations/texas/25-Tex-Admin-Code-SS-229-178
- **Operative text:** 25 TAC §228.31(d): "All food employees, except for the
  certified food protection manager, shall successfully complete an accredited
  food handler training course, within 30 days of employment. This requirement
  does not apply to temporary food establishments." 25 TAC §228.31(e): "The food
  establishment shall maintain on premises a certificate of completion of the
  food handler training course for each food employee." 25 TAC §229.178(d)(1):
  "A food handler certificate issued by an accredited food handler program shall
  be valid for two years." Tex. H&S Code §438.046(b-1): "A food service worker
  trained in a food handler training course that is accredited by the American
  National Standards Institute or that is accredited by the department and listed
  with the registry is considered to have met a local health jurisdiction's
  training, testing, and permitting requirements."
- **Effective date of cited text:** TFER Aug 2021 edition; §229.178 current as
  displayed; statute fetched 2026-07-07.
- **Verified:** 2026-07-07 | **Confidence:** verified
- **Notes:** LEAD CORRECTION — training window is **30 days** after hire per the
  current TFER §228.31(d), NOT 60 days. (The "60-day" figure in older/secondary
  guidance is refuted by the in-force rule text.) §438.046(c) bars a local health
  jurisdiction from charging a fee or issuing a separate local card once an
  accredited card is presented.

### OBL-TX-CATERER-004: TABC permit to sell/serve alcohol (Mixed Beverage Permit + temporary/off-site catering authorization)

- **Applies to:** A caterer that ITSELF sells or serves alcoholic beverages as
  part of its service (holds/provides the alcohol). CONDITIONAL — if the venue or
  a separately-permitted mobile-bar service supplies and serves the alcohol, the
  caterer needs no TABC permit. Permit type depends on what is served: distilled-
  spirit mixed beverages → Mixed Beverage Permit (Alco. Bev. Code ch. 28); beer/
  wine only → the consolidated Wine and Malt Beverage Retailer's permit (not fully
  researched here). Wet areas only — no sales in a dry area even with a permit.
- **Obligation:** Hold the appropriate TABC retail permit; for off-premises events,
  exercise the permit's temporary-location authority (implemented by TABC as the
  "Caterer's Permit" plus a per-event "Catering Certificate").
- **Issuing/enforcing authority:** Texas Alcoholic Beverage Commission (TABC).
- **Cadence:** Two-year term. §11.09(a): a permit "expires on the second
  anniversary of the date it is issued." A secondary/subordinate permit expires
  with its primary (§11.09(b)). Renewal under §11.32. Per-event catering
  certificate: submit at least 10 business days before the event; not issued for
  more than 10 consecutive days (TABC caterer's-permit guidance — `probable`).
- **Penalty for lapse:** Selling without the required permit is prohibited
  (§11.01); TABC may cancel or suspend the permit (§11.61) and pursue
  administrative/criminal penalties. Selling alcohol without a permit is a
  criminal offense.
- **Category:** permit
- **Basis:** regulatory
- **Citation:** Tex. Alcoholic Beverage Code §11.01 (permit required); §28.01
  (mixed beverage permit — authorized activities); §28.19 (sales at temporary
  location = off-site catering); §11.09 (two-year expiration); §11.11 (conduct
  surety bond); §11.32 (renewal). Official text fetched via Playwright browser at
  https://statutes.capitol.texas.gov/Docs/AL/htm/AL.11.htm and
  https://statutes.capitol.texas.gov/Docs/AL/htm/AL.28.htm
- **Operative text:** §11.01(a): "No person who has not first obtained a permit of
  the type required for the privilege exercised may, in a wet area, do any of the
  following: (1) manufacture, distill, brew, sell, possess for the purpose of
  sale, import into this state, export from this state, transport, distribute,
  warehouse, or store liquor..." §28.19(a): "The holder of a mixed beverage permit
  may temporarily sell authorized alcoholic beverages at: (1) a picnic,
  celebration, or similar event; or (2) a place other than the premises for which
  the holder's mixed beverage permit is issued only in [an authorized area]."
  §11.09(a): "A permit issued under this code expires on the second anniversary of
  the date it is issued, except as provided by Subsections (d) and (e) or another
  provision of this code."
- **Effective date of cited text:** Current as displayed (fetched 2026-07-07);
  reflects the HB 1545 (86th Leg., R.S., 2019; most provisions eff. Sept 1, 2021)
  license/permit consolidation. *(Session attribution corrected 2026-07-08 in the
  Pass-5 review — the original entry said "87th Leg., 2021", contradicting the
  cross-agent F-10 correction that pinned HB 1545 = 2019.)*
- **Verified:** 2026-07-07 | **Confidence:** verified
  (MB permit, §28.19 temporary-location catering authority, two-year term and
  conduct surety bond are verified from official statute text; the exact current
  TABC "Caterer's Permit (CB)" letter code and catering-certificate day-counts are
  `probable` — sourced to a 2020 TABC guidance PDF predating full HB 1545 rollout)
- **Notes:** LEAD CONFIRMED — HB 1545 consolidated TABC's ~75 license/permit types
  and moved permits to **two-year** terms (§11.09(a)). The old standalone
  "Caterer's Permit (CB)" is now exercised through the Mixed Beverage Permit's
  §28.19 temporary-location authority. **Conduct surety bond** (§11.11): $5,000 for
  a Chapter 28 (Mixed Beverage) permit ($10,000 within 1,000 ft of a public
  school), waivable after 3+ years' permit tenure without revocation (§11.11(e)),
  substitutable with a CD or letter of credit (§11.11(d)) — see insurance finding
  OBL-TX-CATERER-006. **Many caterers do NOT hold their own alcohol permit** (the
  venue or a licensed bar service does), so this obligation is conditional.

### OBL-TX-CATERER-005: TABC seller-server certification — NOT a statutory mandate (voluntary employer safe-harbor)

- **Applies to:** Employees who sell/serve alcohol for a caterer that holds a TABC
  permit. FINDING: no Texas statute makes seller-server certification a blanket
  legal requirement for a caterer's staff.
- **Obligation:** NONE mandated by statute. TABC seller-server ("TABC
  certification") training is a **voluntary employer safe-harbor**: if the employer
  requires training and the employee actually attends a TABC-approved program (and
  the employer did not encourage the violation), an employee's unlawful-sale
  actions are not attributed to the employer.
- **Issuing/enforcing authority:** TABC (approves seller training programs,
  §106.14(b)).
- **Cadence:** N/A — voluntary. (TABC seller-server certificates are commonly
  valid two years as an administrative matter, not a statutory mandate.)
- **Penalty for lapse:** None — there is no obligation. The consequence of NOT
  training is loss of the §106.14 safe-harbor (potential employer liability for an
  employee's violation).
- **Category:** worker-certification (noted, **NOT encoded** as a legal obligation)
- **Basis:** regulatory (but the program is voluntary — safe-harbor, not mandate)
- **Citation:** Tex. Alcoholic Beverage Code §106.14. Official text fetched via
  Playwright browser at https://statutes.capitol.texas.gov/Docs/AL/htm/AL.106.htm
- **Operative text:** §106.14(a): "...the actions of an employee shall not be
  attributable to the employer if: (1) the employer requires its employees to
  attend a commission-approved seller training program; (2) the employee has
  actually attended such a training program; and (3) the employer has not directly
  or indirectly encouraged the employee to violate such law."
- **Effective date of cited text:** Current as displayed (fetched 2026-07-07).
- **Verified:** 2026-07-07 | **Confidence:** verified (that it is a voluntary
  safe-harbor, not a mandate)
- **Notes:** LEAD RESOLVED — this is a VOLUNTARY safe-harbor, **do NOT encode as a
  required credential**. It is strongly recommended and effectively expected in
  practice, but §106.14 frames it as optional. TABC may, after certain violations,
  order that a permittee's employees become certified (case-specific), and some
  venues/clients contractually require it — situational, not a general statutory
  mandate.

### OBL-TX-CATERER-006: Insurance mandated by law — general/liquor liability NOT mandated (sourced absence); TABC conduct surety bond is the only law-mandated financial assurance

- **Applies to:** All caterers.
- **Obligation / finding:** Texas law does **not** mandate that a caterer carry
  commercial general liability, product liability, or liquor-liability insurance as
  a condition of operating or of holding a food establishment permit. Such COIs are
  **contractual** (venue/client-driven) and are handled by the checklist feature —
  out of regulatory encoding scope per METHODOLOGY §5. The ONLY law-mandated
  financial assurance tied to catering activity is the TABC **conduct surety bond**,
  and only IF the caterer holds a Chapter 28 (Mixed Beverage) permit (§11.11:
  $5,000; $10,000 near a school; waivable after 3 years; CD/letter-of-credit
  substitution allowed).
- **Issuing/enforcing authority:** N/A for insurance (no mandate); TABC for the
  surety bond.
- **Cadence:** Surety bond maintained while the Chapter 28 permit is held (subject
  to the §11.11(e) 3-year waiver).
- **Penalty for lapse:** N/A for insurance. Failure to file a required conduct
  surety bond blocks issuance/renewal of the Mixed Beverage permit.
- **Category:** insurance
- **Basis:** regulatory (absence for liability insurance; the surety bond is a
  regulatory, conditional financial-assurance requirement)
- **Citation:** No insurance mandate found in Tex. Health & Safety Code ch. 437 /
  ch. 438 or the TFER (25 TAC ch. 228), reviewed in-session; TABC conduct surety
  bond at Tex. Alcoholic Beverage Code §11.11
  (https://statutes.capitol.texas.gov/Docs/AL/htm/AL.11.htm).
- **Operative text:** §11.11(a): "...an applicant for a permit or a holder of a
  permit issued under: (1) Chapter 25, 28, or 32 of this code shall file with the
  commission a surety bond in the amount of $5,000 conditioned on the applicant's
  or holder's conformance with alcoholic beverage law..." §11.11(e): "A holder of a
  permit issued under this code who has held a permit for three years or more
  before the date the holder applied for renewal of the permit is not required to
  furnish a surety bond if the holder [meets the no-revocation / continuous-
  operation conditions]."
- **Effective date of cited text:** Current as displayed (fetched 2026-07-07).
- **Verified:** 2026-07-07 | **Confidence:** verified (absence of a caterer
  liability-insurance mandate within the reviewed food + alcohol regulatory corpus;
  surety-bond text verified)
- **Notes:** Texas **dram-shop liability** (Alco. Bev. Code ch. 2) creates tort
  exposure for over-service but is NOT an insurance mandate. Workers' compensation
  is OPTIONAL for private Texas employers (Labor Code ch. 406) — cross-cutting,
  owned by the venue-org research; a non-subscribing caterer-employer must file
  DWC notices — cross-reference only, not encoded here. Auto financial
  responsibility for delivery vehicles (Transportation Code ch. 601) is a general
  driver requirement, not caterer-specific.

### OBL-TX-CATERER-007: DSHS Mobile Food Vendor license (statewide, ch. 437B) — REQUIRED only if the caterer dispenses food/beverage from a food vending vehicle (food truck/trailer), effective 2026-07-01

- **Applies to:** A caterer ONLY IF it operates as a "mobile food vendor" — i.e., it
  "dispenses food or beverages from a food vending vehicle for immediate service or
  consumption" (§437B.001(5)), where a "food vending vehicle" is "any vehicle that
  operates as a food service establishment and is designed to be readily movable"
  (§437B.001(2)). CONDITIONAL / NARROW: this captures a caterer that runs a food
  truck, concession trailer, or mobile grill/cart from which food is served directly
  to guests on-site. It does NOT capture the common caterer that merely transports
  prepared food to a venue in a delivery van and plates/serves it from the venue's
  kitchen or buffet — that van is not "operating as a food service establishment"
  dispensing for immediate consumption, so such a caterer is a (fixed) food
  establishment under OBL-TX-CATERER-001 / the local permit, NOT a mobile food
  vendor. A SEPARATE license is required for EACH food vending vehicle operated.
- **Obligation:** Hold a current DSHS Mobile Food Vendor license for each food
  vending vehicle. Statewide and MANDATORY (not residual): a mobile food vendor may
  not operate anywhere in Texas without the DSHS license, regardless of any local
  permit.
- **Issuing/enforcing authority:** Texas Department of State Health Services (DSHS)
  ("the department"). A local authority may conduct the health inspections only under
  a collaborative agreement with DSHS (§437B.153); it does not issue the license.
- **Cadence:** Annual. §437B.055(b): a license "expires on the first anniversary of
  the date of issuance." Renewal application due before expiration; the vendor "may
  continue to operate while the vendor's application for renewal is pending"
  (§437B.056). (DSHS program page, agency practice: the license "expires one year
  from the date of your successfully completed pre-licensing inspection.")
- **Penalty for lapse:** Operating as a mobile food vendor without the required DSHS
  license is prohibited (§437B.051(a)). DSHS may deny, suspend, or revoke a license
  (§437B.202); a license holder who continues to operate after suspension/revocation
  is subject to an administrative penalty in an amount DSHS determines (§437B.205).
  (Ch. 437B does not carry ch. 437's Class A-misdemeanor clause — the chapter's
  express monetary sanction is the §437B.205 administrative penalty; see Notes.)
- **Category:** license
- **Basis:** regulatory
- **Citation:** Tex. Health & Safety Code ch. 437B — §437B.001 (definitions),
  §437B.002 (construction), §437B.003 (local preemption), §437B.051 (license
  required), §437B.055 (issuance/term), §437B.056 (renewal), §437B.101 (compliance
  with state & local law), §437B.151 (classifications), §437B.202/§437B.205
  (enforcement) — added by Acts 2025, 89th Leg., R.S., Ch. 744 (H.B. 2844), §2,
  eff. July 1, 2026. Official codified text fetched via Playwright browser at
  https://statutes.capitol.texas.gov/Docs/HS/htm/HS.437B.htm ; official enrolled bill
  (capitol.texas.gov, Texas Legislature Online) at
  https://capitol.texas.gov/tlodocs/89R/billtext/pdf/HB02844F.pdf ; DSHS program page
  https://www.dshs.texas.gov/retail-food-establishments/permits-retail-food-establishments/mobile-food-vendors
- **Operative text:** §437B.051(a): "A person may not operate as a mobile food vendor
  in this state unless the person holds a mobile food vendor's license issued by the
  department. A separate license is required for each food vending vehicle a mobile
  food vendor operates." §437B.001(5): "'Mobile food vendor' means any person who
  dispenses food or beverages from a food vending vehicle for immediate service or
  consumption." §437B.001(2): "'Food vending vehicle' means any vehicle that operates
  as a food service establishment and is designed to be readily movable." §437B.055(b):
  "A license issued under this section expires on the first anniversary of the date of
  issuance." §437B.003: "This chapter preempts a local authority's power to prohibit

- **Effective date of cited text:** Ch. 437B added eff. **July 1, 2026** (only
  §437B.004 rules-authority took effect earlier, Sept 1, 2025); DSHS implementing
  rules due by May 1, 2026; no license required before July 1, 2026 (HB 2844 §5(c),
  §6). Codified text current as displayed (fetched 2026-07-07, 6 days after effect).
- **Verified:** 2026-07-07 | **Confidence:** verified | **Provenance:** official
  (codified ch. 437B read on statutes.capitol.texas.gov via the Playwright browser
  AND the enrolled bill HB02844F on capitol.texas.gov — the two agree verbatim; DSHS

  and into this dedicated statewide DSHS regime, and repealed §437A.003 (the old
  populous-county-airport mobile-establishment permit). Interaction with local
  authority = COEXIST-with-state-primacy, NOT residual: the DSHS license is
  affirmatively required statewide; local authorities keep NON-conflicting powers
  (zoning, fire codes, on-site location restrictions per §437B.101) and may keep a
  non-conflicting local MFV program (§437B.002), but may not prohibit a state-licensed
  vendor or impose conflicting / more-restrictive rules (§437B.003, §437B.051(b)).
  DSHS classifies vendors by rule into **Type I / II / III** for inspection frequency
  (§437B.151 — Type I prepackaged/low-risk; Type II limited handling; Type III
  prepare/cook/hold/serve from the vehicle); several 2025-era secondary sources cited
  only "Type I/II" — the enacted statute has three. A mobile food vendor must STILL
  satisfy ch. 438 food-manager/handler certification (§437B.104), so
  OBL-TX-CATERER-002 and -003 continue to apply to its staff. "The department" = DSHS
  (Title 6 Health & Safety Code usage; DSHS program page confirms). The exact DSHS fee
  schedule and the FINAL Type I/II/III food-category rules are set by
  executive-commissioner rule (due May 1, 2026) and were not fetched verbatim
  in-session — see Open questions. The statutory license requirement, issuer, annual
  term and preemption posture are verified.

## Local-level obligations (noted, not encoded)

- **Local city/county/public-health-district food establishment permit.** This is
  the operative permit for most caterers. Under H&S Code §§437.003–437.004, a
  county or public health district may require a food permit and inspections; many
  Texas cities (Home-Rule) run their own health departments (e.g. Austin/Travis
  County, City of Houston, City of Dallas, San Antonio Metro Health, Fort Worth /
  Tarrant County). A caterer's commissary/base of operations is typically permitted
  and inspected locally, and each catered event may require a Temporary Food
  Establishment permit from the local jurisdiction (25 TAC §§228.221–228.222).
  DSHS state permitting only fills the gap where no local authority permits.
  Product guidance: "check your city/county health department."
- **Temporary Food Establishment (TFE) permit per event.** Off-site catered events
  can trigger a per-event TFE permit from the local health authority (25 TAC
  §228.222). Not encoded (local + event-specific).
- **Certificate of Occupancy / fire marshal / grease-trap / commissary approval**
  for the caterer's kitchen — local building & fire codes. Not encoded.
- **Food truck / mobile food unit → now a STATE license (ENCODED), not local, as of
  2026-07-01.** A caterer that operates a food truck / concession trailer dispensing
  food to guests on-site is a "mobile food vendor" and must hold the statewide DSHS
  license under H&S ch. 437B — this is now ENCODED as **OBL-TX-CATERER-007** (HB 2844
  struck "mobile food unit" from §437.0055 and repealed §437A.003, effective
  2026-07-01). What REMAINS local-and-not-encoded for such a vehicle: the
  non-conflicting zoning, fire-code, and on-site location restrictions the vendor must
  still satisfy under §437B.101, plus any non-conflicting local MFV program a city
  retains under §437B.002. The pre-2026 framing ("25 TAC §228.221 local MFU
  permitting") is superseded for the state-license question; a caterer that only
  transports and serves prepared food at a venue is NOT a mobile food vendor and is
  governed by the fixed-establishment permit (OBL-TX-CATERER-001 / local food permit).
