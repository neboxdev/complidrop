# Federal regulatory obligations — CATERER (food & beverage catering serving event venues)

Entity type: `caterer`. Jurisdiction: United States federal (`federal`). Scope per
METHODOLOGY.md: business/activity licenses & permits, per-worker certifications,
insurance where mandated by law. W-9/tax forms deferred.

Headline: **there is essentially no federal license or permit for an ordinary
retail caterer.** Food-safety operating requirements and food handler/manager
certifications are imposed by STATE law (for Texas, see
`docs/rules-research/texas/caterer.md`). The one genuine federal obligation is a
TTB **alcohol dealer registration** — and only for a caterer that itself serves/
sells alcoholic beverages. Everything else is written below as a sourced ABSENCE
finding.

---

### OBL-FED-CATERER-001: FDA Food Facility Registration — caterers are EXEMPT (sourced absence)

- **Applies to:** A caterer as a "restaurant"/"retail food establishment." FINDING:
  such establishments are expressly EXEMPT from FDA food facility registration. A
  caterer therefore does NOT register its catering operation with the FDA.
- **Obligation:** NONE (exempt). (Contrast: food manufacturers/processors,
  packers, and holders must register biennially under 21 U.S.C. 350d; caterers are
  outside that regime.)
- **Issuing/enforcing authority:** U.S. Food and Drug Administration (FDA).
- **Cadence:** N/A (no registration required).
- **Penalty for lapse:** N/A (no obligation).
- **Category:** license (finding of non-applicability)
- **Basis:** regulatory (absence)
- **Citation:** 21 CFR 1.226(c),(d) and 21 CFR 1.227 (Registration of Food
  Facilities, Subpart H). Official eCFR text fetched via browser at
  https://www.ecfr.gov/current/title-21/chapter-I/subchapter-A/part-1/subpart-H
- **Operative text:** 21 CFR 1.226: "This subpart does not apply to the following
  facilities: ... (c) Retail food establishments; (d) Restaurants; ..." 21 CFR
  1.227 (definition of Restaurant): "Restaurant means a facility that prepares and
  sells food directly to consumers for immediate consumption... (1) Entities in
  which food is provided to humans, such as cafeterias, lunchrooms, cafes,
  bistros, fast food establishments, food stands, saloons, taverns, bars,
  lounges, catering facilities, hospital kitchens, day care kitchens, and nursing
  home kitchens are restaurants..." (emphasis: "catering facilities" are
  restaurants). 21 CFR 1.227 (Retail food establishment): "Retail food
  establishment means an establishment that sells food products directly to
  consumers as its primary function."
- **Effective date of cited text:** Current as displayed (eCFR current edition,
  fetched 2026-07-07).
- **Verified:** 2026-07-07 | **Confidence:** verified
- **Notes:** NUANCE — a "central kitchen" or commissary that only prepares food
  for distribution to OTHER facilities and does NOT "prepare and serve food
  directly to consumers for immediate consumption" is NOT a "restaurant" under
  §1.227 and could fall into the food-facility-registration regime. A typical
  event caterer that plates/serves directly to guests is a restaurant/retail food
  establishment and is exempt. Because caterers are retail, FSMA Preventive
  Controls (21 CFR part 117) and FDA food-facility registration do not apply.

### OBL-FED-CATERER-002: TTB Alcohol Dealer Registration (TTB Form 5630.5d) — REQUIRED if the caterer serves/sells alcohol

- **Applies to:** A caterer that sells OR serves distilled spirits, wine, or beer
  to customers — including serving liquor with meals to paying guests EVEN IF no
  separate charge is made for the alcohol. CONDITIONAL: does not apply to a
  caterer that never handles alcohol (where the venue or a separately-licensed bar
  service provides it).
- **Obligation:** Register as an alcohol dealer with the federal Alcohol and
  Tobacco Tax and Trade Bureau (TTB) by filing TTB Form 5630.5d before engaging in
  business. (There is no longer a federal Special Occupational Tax — it was
  repealed effective July 1, 2008 — but the REGISTRATION requirement remains.)
- **Issuing/enforcing authority:** U.S. Alcohol and Tobacco Tax and Trade Bureau
  (TTB), Department of the Treasury.
- **Cadence:** File TTB Form 5630.5d before engaging in business; thereafter on or
  before July 1 of each year — BUT no additional filing is required so long as none
  of the information on the form has changed since the last filing (so in practice
  a one-time registration, re-filed only when information changes).
- **Penalty for lapse:** Federal penalties apply under the Internal Revenue Code
  (26 U.S.C.) for engaging in business as a dealer without registering / for
  recordkeeping failures. (Specific penalty section not fetched in-session — see
  Open questions.)
- **Category:** permit (registration)
- **Basis:** regulatory
- **Citation:** 27 CFR 31.31 (retail dealer in liquors — registration required);
  27 CFR 31.42 (restaurants/persons serving liquors with meals are dealers);
  27 CFR 31.111 (registration form due date; TTB F 5630.5d); 26 U.S.C. 5122, 5124.
  Official eCFR text fetched via browser at
  https://www.ecfr.gov/current/title-27/chapter-I/subchapter-A/part-31/subpart-C
  and https://www.ecfr.gov/current/title-27/chapter-I/subchapter-A/part-31/subpart-G/section-31.111
- **Operative text:** 27 CFR 31.31(a): "Except as otherwise provided in paragraph
  (b) of this section, every person who sells or offers for sale distilled
  spirits, wines, or beer to any person other than a dealer is a retail dealer in
  liquors for purposes of this part. Every retail dealer in liquors must comply
  with the registration and other requirements of this part, unless the dealer is
  covered by an applicable exemption under subpart D of this part." 27 CFR 31.42:
  "Proprietors of restaurants and other persons who serve liquors with meals to
  paying customers, even if no separate or specific charge for the liquors is
  made, are dealers subject to the provisions of this part." 27 CFR 31.111(a):
  "Dealers must register by filing the registration form, TTB Form 5630.5d, before
  engaging in business and on or before July 1 of each year thereafter. However,
  as long as none of the information specified on the form has changed since the
  previous registration form was filed, no additional registration is required."
- **Effective date of cited text:** Current as displayed (eCFR current edition,
  fetched 2026-07-07). Special Occupational Tax repealed eff. July 1, 2008 (Pub. L.
  109-59 §11125).
- **Verified:** 2026-07-07 | **Confidence:** verified
- **Notes:** This is FEDERAL registration and is SEPARATE from (and additional to)
  the Texas TABC permit (see OBL-TX-CATERER-004). It is a genuine, frequently-
  overlooked federal obligation. There is no fee for the registration itself
  (the occupational tax is $0/repealed), but the filing is mandatory. A caterer
  who never serves alcohol has no TTB obligation.

### OBL-FED-CATERER-003: No federal operating license and no federal per-worker food certification (sourced absence)

- **Applies to:** All caterers (as retail food service).
- **Obligation / finding:** No U.S. federal agency issues an operating license, a
  business permit, or a per-worker food-safety certification for an ordinary retail
  caterer. Food-establishment permitting and food handler / certified food
  manager credentials are creatures of STATE law (Texas: Health & Safety Code
  ch. 437/438; 25 TAC ch. 228/229 — see the Texas dossier). The FDA **Food Code**
  is a *model* code that the FDA publishes for voluntary adoption by state and
  local jurisdictions; it is not itself federal law and imposes no direct federal
  obligation on a caterer.
- **Issuing/enforcing authority:** N/A (no federal licensor). FDA (model Food Code
  only); USDA/FSIS (meat & poultry — retail-exempt, see Notes).
- **Cadence:** N/A.
- **Penalty for lapse:** N/A.
- **Category:** license / worker-certification (finding of non-applicability)
- **Basis:** regulatory (absence)
- **Citation:** 21 CFR 1.226(d) (caterers exempt from even FDA registration —
  demonstrating no federal retail-food licensing of caterers); absence of any
  federal food-service licensing statute. eCFR fetched via browser at
  https://www.ecfr.gov/current/title-21/chapter-I/subchapter-A/part-1/subpart-H
- **Operative text:** 21 CFR 1.226: "This subpart does not apply to the following
  facilities: ... (d) Restaurants; ..." (The FDA does not even require caterers to
  register, let alone hold a federal food-service license; there is no federal
  food handler/manager certification.)
- **Effective date of cited text:** Current as displayed (fetched 2026-07-07).
- **Verified:** 2026-07-07 | **Confidence:** verified (for the absence of any
  federal license/worker-certification for retail catering). The "Food Code is a
  model, not federal law" statement is well-established FDA context but was not
  quoted verbatim from the FDA Food Code page in-session — treat that specific
  characterization as `probable`; the ABSENCE finding itself is verified.
- **Notes:** USDA/FSIS regulates meat & poultry PROCESSING; a retail caterer that
  merely cooks/serves USDA-inspected meat is within the retail exemption and is not
  a federally-inspected establishment (cf. 21 CFR 1.226(g)). OSHA workplace-safety
  rules and federal employment law (FLSA, EEOC, IRS employment tax) apply to a
  caterer as an employer generally, but are cross-cutting employer obligations, not
  caterer-specific licenses/certifications — out of encoding scope. Federal
  chain-restaurant menu-labeling (21 CFR 101.11) applies only to establishments
  that are part of a chain of 20+ locations — not a typical caterer.

### OBL-FED-CATERER-004: Insurance mandated by federal law — none for caterers (sourced absence)

- **Applies to:** All caterers.
- **Obligation / finding:** No federal law mandates that a caterer carry general
  liability, product liability, liquor-liability, or any other insurance. (General
  liability / liquor-liability COIs a venue may demand are CONTRACTUAL, handled by
  the checklist feature — out of regulatory scope per METHODOLOGY §5.)
- **Issuing/enforcing authority:** N/A.
- **Cadence:** N/A.
- **Penalty for lapse:** N/A.
- **Category:** insurance (finding of non-applicability)
- **Basis:** regulatory (absence)
- **Citation:** Absence — no federal insurance-mandate statute applies to retail
  food catering. (Federal insurance mandates that exist — e.g., FMCSA/DOT motor-
  carrier liability minimums under 49 CFR part 387, or workers' compensation for
  maritime/federal workers — do not reach an ordinary local caterer.)
- **Operative text:** N/A (absence finding — no operative text exists to quote).
- **Effective date of cited text:** N/A.
- **Verified:** 2026-07-07 | **Confidence:** verified (absence)
- **Notes:** Workers' compensation is governed by STATE law (Texas: optional under
  Labor Code ch. 406 — owned by the venue-org research). Commercial-auto financial
  responsibility for delivery vehicles is STATE law (Texas Transportation Code
  ch. 601). FMCSA insurance minimums (49 CFR 387) attach only to for-hire motor
  carriers operating qualifying commercial motor vehicles in interstate commerce —
  a caterer's ordinary catering van is not such a carrier. None of these is a
  federal insurance mandate specific to catering.
